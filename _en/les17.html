--- order: 617 title: Lesson 17 subtitle: - Verb tense forms ---

We already know three tense forms of the Abkhazian language:
present I (static verbs) -уп
present II (dynamic verbs) -оит\\-уеит
past perfect -ит.

Let's get acquainted with other tense forms.

Past imperfect tense is formed using the suffix –он: иҟаиҵон he was doing, исфон I was eating.
In the past perfect (with suffix -ит) we have a completed action, a final result. In the past imperfect there is no final result.

Remember the proverb!
{% include audio.html file="17-1" %} «Ан лыҧҳа' дла'бжьон, аҧҳа' а'мҵқәа лыҧхьаӡо'н. The mother was teaching her daughter, and the daughter was counting flies.

Past indefinite tense is formed using the suffix -н
This tense form is almost never used independently in a sentence. It is used in connection with another tense form. Its non-independence arises from the incompleteness of the meaning it gives to the verb.
Cf.: изга'н I took and...; ибфа'н you (f) ate and...

Future tense is formed using the suffix -п: иҟасҵа'п I will do; исфа'п I will eat; изҩы'п I will write, сца'п I will go.

Reminder! If the verb stem ends in -а then according to the phonetic rules of the Abkhazian language the combination -aya (a+suffix -уа(ит)) gives -уо or –o. (a+ya//ay+a = oy//o).
The combinations иa, aи, аиа give variants иe, eи, e.

Table of tense forms
Tense forms suffixes examples
present static -уп сгылоуп
present dynamic -оит, -уеит бгылоит, быҧшуеит
past perfect -ит уаҧхьеит, уҧшит
past imperfect -он ибон, ифон
past indefinite -н илбан, илфан
future -п иҟасҵап, исфап
