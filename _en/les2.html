Lesson 2

Contents:
Personal pronouns; singular
Work with vocabulary

Мшыбзи'а! Hello!
We are about to start lesson 2.

Abkhazian language doesn't have gender, but it does have class: animate class (personal); and inanimate class. Animate class only relates to people! Everything else, including and living and lifeless natural objects, belongs to the inanimate class. The animate class is subdivided into subclasses feminine and masculine.
Personal pronouns in Abkhazian have a full form and a short form.

Singular, animate, personal pronouns

First person сара' - short form с(а) - I
Second person уара' - short form у(а) - you (masculine)
бара' - short form б(а) - you (feminine)
Third person иара' - short form и(а), д(а) - he
лара' - short form л(а), д(а) - she

Remember that the brackets show an optional part.

New words and expressions (and their conjugation elements).

Try and remember them!

ацара' (infinite form of verb) to go; to leave.

To make it easier to remember the conjugation rules, we will look at the structure of Abkhazian verbs.

а-ца-ра - а- prefix of the general (dictionary) form, ца- root, -ра - suffix. When conjugating, the prefix а- and suffx -ра are separated.

Сара сцоит - I am going. Here the verb сцо'ит is made up of с(а) - I, ц(а) - root "to go", -оит - suffix in the present tense.

сара' сцо'ит - I am going
бара' бцо'ит - you (a woman) are going
уара' уцо'ит - you (a man) are going
иара' дцо'ит - he is going
лара' дцо'ит - she is going

Notice how the verbs reflect the subject:
сара' сцо'ит, уара' уцо'ит, бара' бцо'ит.

The conjugated verb takes into itself an indicator of the subject.

абара' - to see
сара' избо'ит - I see
бара' иббо'ит - you (a woman) see
уара' иубо'ит - you (a man) see
иара' (и)ибо'ит - he sees
лара' илбо'ит - she sees

агара' - to take
сара' изго'ит - I take
бара' ибго'ит - you (a woman) takes
уара' иуго'ит - you (a man) takes
иара' (и)иго'ит - he takes
лара' илго'ит - she takes

The first and third person singular prefix is often dropped. However, even when it's not used, we'll add it in brackets, so that it's easier to understand.
Before voiced consonants, the first person indicator с(а) changes to з(а), for example, изгоит (исгоит "I take").

Remember these words!

ацара' - to go, to leave. Сара' сцо'ит - I am going.
аара' - to come. Бара' баауе'ит - you (a woman) are coming.
абара' - to see. Лара' илбо'ит - she sees.
агара' - to take. Иара' (и)иго'ит - he takes.
ара' (абра') - here.
ана' - there.
бзи'а абара' - to love. Сара' бзи'а бызбо'ит - I love you (to a woman). Сара' бзи'а узбо'ит - I love you (to a man). Сара' сан бзи'а дызбо'ит - I love my mother
анхара' - to live. Лара' ара' дынхо'ит - she lives here. Иара' ана' дынхо'ит - he lives there.
ау'сура - work
амшы'н - the sea. Сара' амшы'н ахь сцо'ит - I am going to the sea.
