Abkhazian language doesn't have gender, but it does have class: animate class (personal); and inanimate class. Animate class only relates to people! Everything else, including and living and lifeless natural objects, belongs to the inanimate class. The animate class is subdivided into subclasses feminine and masculine.
Personal pronouns in Abkhazian have a full form and a short form.

Singular, animate, personal pronouns

First person —Ā–į—Ä–įŐĀ - short form —Ā(–į) - I
Second person —É–į—Ä–įŐĀ - short form —É(–į) - you (masculine)
–Ī–į—Ä–įŐĀ - short form –Ī(–į) - you (feminine)
Third person –ł–į—Ä–įŐĀ - short form –ł(–į), –ī(–į) - he
–Ľ–į—Ä–įŐĀ - short form –Ľ(–į), –ī(–į) - she

Remember that the brackets show an optional part.

New words and expressions (and their conjugation elements).

Try and remember them!

–į—Ü–į—Ä–įŐĀ (infinite form of verb) to go; to leave.

To make it easier to remember the conjugation rules, we will look at the structure of Abkhazian verbs.

–į-—Ü–į-—Ä–į - –į- prefix of the general (dictionary) form, —Ü–į- root, -—Ä–į - suffix. When conjugating, the prefix –į- and suffx -—Ä–į are separated.

–°–į—Ä–į —Ā—Ü–ĺ–ł—ā - I am going. Here the verb —Ā—Ü–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā is made up of —Ā(–į) - I, —Ü(–į) - root "to go", -–ĺ–ł—ā - suffix in the present tense.

{% include audio.html file="2-2" %} —Ā–į—Ä–įŐĀ —Ā—Ü–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - I am going
–Ī–į—Ä–įŐĀ –Ī—Ü–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - you (a woman) are going
—É–į—Ä–įŐĀ —É—Ü–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - you (a man) are going
–ł–į—Ä–įŐĀ –ī—Ü–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - he is going
–Ľ–į—Ä–įŐĀ –ī—Ü–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - she is going

Notice how the verbs reflect the subject:
—Ā–į—Ä–įŐĀ —Ā—Ü–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā, —É–į—Ä–įŐĀ —É—Ü–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā, –Ī–į—Ä–įŐĀ –Ī—Ü–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā.

The conjugated verb takes into itself an indicator of the subject.

{% include audio.html file="2-3" %} –į–Ī–į—Ä–įŐĀ - to see
—Ā–į—Ä–įŐĀ –ł–∑–Ī–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - I see
–Ī–į—Ä–įŐĀ –ł–Ī–Ī–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - you (a woman) see
—É–į—Ä–įŐĀ –ł—É–Ī–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - you (a man) see
–ł–į—Ä–įŐĀ (–ł)–ł–Ī–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - he sees
–Ľ–į—Ä–įŐĀ –ł–Ľ–Ī–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - she sees

{% include audio.html file="2-4" %} –į–≥–į—Ä–įŐĀ - to take
—Ā–į—Ä–įŐĀ –ł–∑–≥–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - I take
–Ī–į—Ä–įŐĀ –ł–Ī–≥–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - you (a woman) takes
—É–į—Ä–įŐĀ –ł—É–≥–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - you (a man) takes
–ł–į—Ä–įŐĀ (–ł)–ł–≥–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - he takes
–Ľ–į—Ä–įŐĀ –ł–Ľ–≥–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - she takes

The first and third person singular prefix is often dropped. However, even when it's not used, we'll add it in brackets, so that it's easier to understand.
Before voiced consonants, the first person indicator —Ā(–į) changes to –∑(–į), for example, –ł–∑–≥–ĺ–ł—ā (–ł—Ā–≥–ĺ–ł—ā "I take").

Remember these words!

–į—Ü–į—Ä–įŐĀ - to go, to leave. –°–į—Ä–įŐĀ —Ā—Ü–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - I am going.
–į–į—Ä–įŐĀ - to come. –Ď–į—Ä–įŐĀ –Ī–į–į—É–ĶŐĀ–ł—ā - you (a woman) are coming.

–į–Ĺ–įŐĀ - there.
–Ī–∑–łŐĀ–į –į–Ī–į—Ä–įŐĀ - to love. –°–į—Ä–įŐĀ –Ī–∑–łŐĀ–į –Ī—č–∑–Ī–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - I love you (to a woman). –°–į—Ä–įŐĀ –Ī–∑–łŐĀ–į —É–∑–Ī–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - I love you (to a man). –°–į—Ä–įŐĀ —Ā–į–Ĺ –Ī–∑–łŐĀ–į –ī—č–∑–Ī–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - I love my mother

–į—ÉŐĀ—Ā—É—Ä–į - work
–į–ľ—ą—čŐĀ–Ĺ - the sea. –°–į—Ä–įŐĀ –į–ľ—ą—čŐĀ–Ĺ –į—Ö—Ć —Ā—Ü–ĺŐĀ–ł—ā - I am going to the sea.
